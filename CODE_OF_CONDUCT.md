# Redot Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

The sole purpose of the Redot project is to create the best possible game engine. 

As contributors, we focus our efforts on technical quality, stability, performance, and usability. 
We welcome anyone who wants to help advance that goal. We judge contributions and ideas on their technical 
merit and usefulness to the project, not on the personal characteristics or opinions of the contributor. We 
do not use our roles in the project to target, isolate, or punish people we disagree with on matters unrelated to the engine.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contribute to a healthy project environment:

* Treating other contributors with courtesy and respect
* Being receptive to technical feedback and differing approaches to solving problems
* Giving and receiving constructive criticism focused on the work, not the person
* Taking responsibility for mistakes, apologizing when appropriate, and correcting them
* Prioritizing what is best for the long-term health and quality of the engine

Examples of unacceptable behavior:

* Sexualized language or imagery, and any form of sexual attention or advances
* Trolling, insulting, or derogatory comments directed at other contributors
* Public or private harassment
* Publishing others' private information (physical address, email, etc.) without explicit permission
* Using one's position in the project to target or punish others over personal, ideological, or political disagreements
* Introducing unrelated political or ideological topics into project discussions and decision-making
* Any other conduct that interferes with the project's goal of building an excellent game engine

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for enforcing these standards in a fair and consistent manner. 
They will take appropriate action in response to any behavior they deem harmful to the project.

Maintainers have the right to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code changes, 
issues, pull requests, or other contributions that do not align with this Code of Conduct.
When doing so, they will provide a clear explanation of the decision when appropriate.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all official Redot community spaces, including the repository, issue tracker,
forums, chat channels, and official events. It also applies when an individual is representing the project publicly 
(using official email addresses, social media accounts, or acting as a designated representative).

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive or unacceptable behavior may be reported to the project maintainers responsible for enforcement.

All reports will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly. Project maintainers are required to respect the privacy and security of the reporter.

## Enforcement Guidelines

Project maintainers will follow these guidelines when determining consequences:

### 1. Correction
**Impact**: Use of inappropriate language or unprofessional behavior.  
**Consequence**: A private written warning explaining the violation. A public apology may be requested.

### 2. Warning
**Impact**: A single serious violation or repeated inappropriate behavior.  
**Consequence**: A formal warning with clear consequences for continued violations. This may include a temporary prohibition
on interaction with affected parties. Continued violations may result in a temporary or permanent ban.

### 3. Temporary Ban
**Impact**: Serious or sustained violation of project standards.  
**Consequence**: A temporary ban from all project interaction and communication for a specified period. No public or private interaction with the people involved is permitted during this time.

### 4. Permanent Ban
**Impact**: Repeated violations, sustained harassment, or behavior that clearly damages the project's ability to function.  
**Consequence**: Permanent ban from all public interaction within the Redot project.
